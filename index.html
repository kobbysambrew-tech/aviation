Ghana National Air

Launching two ATR 72-600 aircraft, fully Ghanaian-owned. Connecting domestic and regional destinations while keeping profits in Ghana. Join us in building the nation's pride in aviation.

Our Mission

Ghana National Air provides a fully Ghanaian-owned airline that strengthens domestic and regional connectivity. Profits stay in Ghana, empowering investors, stimulating economic growth, creating jobs, and promoting tourism. Transparency ensures trust, and passengers experience world-class service.

The Opportunity

Domestic air travel is underserved and foreign carriers dominate. Many cities have limited connectivity. Rising economic activity, urbanization, and regional demand make this the perfect moment to launch a fully Ghanaian-owned airline.

Our Solution

Operating ATR 72-600 aircraft for domestic and regional routes, Ghana National Air enables shareholders to enjoy profit participation and governance rights. Local investment retains economic value, builds aviation expertise, and ensures a sustainable airline.

Why ATR 72-600

Cost

New aircraft cost $29–32M; 1–2 year old planes $24–28M. Low acquisition costs enable a cost-efficient startup fleet without sacrificing modern technology.

Capacity & Range

Seats 68–78 passengers, with a range up to 1,500 km. Perfect for domestic and regional flights connecting key cities efficiently.

Efficiency

Fuel-efficient engines reduce operational costs. Minimal maintenance downtime ensures maximum availability and profitability.

Aircraft Interior

Spacious, comfortable seating for 68–78 passengers with modern amenities. Boarding and disembarkation optimized for efficiency. Ensures superior passenger experience on every flight.

Fleet Plan

Start with two new or 1–2 year old ATR 72-600 aircraft. Expand fleet as demand grows. Strategic maintenance and optimized scheduling ensure safety, reliability, and maximum revenue generation.

Domestic Routes

Initial routes include Accra ↔ Kumasi, Accra ↔ Tamale, and Accra ↔ Takoradi. 2–3 daily flights maximize revenue while providing essential connectivity. Efficient scheduling reduces costs and improves customer satisfaction.

Regional Routes

Expansion includes Accra ↔ Abidjan, Accra ↔ Lomé, Accra ↔ Lagos, eventually covering ECOWAS destinations. Routes selected for profitability and high demand, ensuring strong early adoption.

Operational Timeline

  1. 0–6 months: Raise equity, regulatory approvals, finalize aircraft selection.
  2. 6–12 months: Recruit/train staff, safety certifications, operational dashboards.
  3. 12–18 months: Launch flights, establish brand presence, optimize operations.

Shareholder Model

100% Ghanaian ownership. Shareholders enjoy voting rights, profit participation, and transparent quarterly reporting. Retains national wealth while promoting accountability.

Governance & Transparency

Independent board oversees operations and finances. Real-time dashboards, quarterly audits, and transparent reporting eliminate corruption and build investor confidence.

Investor Protections

Capital released in milestones. Major decisions require shareholder approval. Escrow accounts for significant expenses ensure accountability and mitigate risk.
