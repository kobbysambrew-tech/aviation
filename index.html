Ghana National Air

Launching two ATR 72-600 aircraft, fully Ghanaian-owned. Connecting domestic and regional destinations while keeping profits in Ghana.

Our Mission

Ghana National Air aims to provide a fully Ghanaian-owned airline that strengthens domestic and regional connectivity. Retaining profits locally empowers investors and stimulates economic growth. Our airline creates jobs, enhances tourism, and serves as a symbol of national pride.

The Opportunity

Domestic air travel is underserved. Foreign carriers dominate, and many cities lack connectivity. Rising demand and economic growth make this an ideal time for a fully Ghanaian-owned airline to capture the market.

Our Solution

Operating ATR 72-600 aircraft, we offer reliable domestic and regional flights. Shareholders benefit from profits and decision-making power. Ghana National Air strengthens the aviation sector while keeping wealth in Ghana.

Why ATR 72-600

Cost

New aircraft cost $29–32M; 1–2 year old planes $24–28M. Low acquisition costs for a modern start-up fleet.

Capacity & Range

Seats 68–78 passengers, range up to 1,500 km, ideal for domestic and regional flights.

Efficiency

Fuel-efficient, minimal maintenance downtime, optimized for short-haul operations.

Aircraft Interior

Comfortable seating for 68–78 passengers, modern avionics, and efficient boarding. Optimized for passenger comfort and operational reliability on short-haul flights.

Fleet Plan

Start with two new or 1–2 year old aircraft. Expand gradually as demand grows. Strategic maintenance ensures maximum uptime and safety.

Domestic Routes

Initial routes: Accra ↔ Kumasi, Accra ↔ Tamale, Accra ↔ Takoradi. 2–3 daily flights per route maximize revenue and passenger access.

Regional Routes

Accra ↔ Abidjan, Accra ↔ Lomé, Accra ↔ Lagos initially. ECOWAS expansion planned. Optimized frequency ensures profitability while meeting demand.

Operational Timeline

  1. 0–6 months: Raise equity, regulatory approvals, finalize aircraft selection.
  2. 6–12 months: Recruit/train staff, safety certifications, dashboards.
  3. 12–18 months: Launch flights, establish brand, optimize operations.

Shareholder Model

100% Ghanaian ownership. Shareholders have voting rights, profit participation, and quarterly reports. Profits remain in Ghana.

Governance & Transparency

Independent board oversees operations and finances. Quarterly audits, live dashboards, and transparency eliminate corruption.

Investor Protections

Capital released in tranches tied to milestones. Key decisions require shareholder approval. Escrow for major expenses ensures accountability.

Revenue Model

Ticket sales on domestic and regional routes. Low operating costs with ATR 72-600, profit-sharing with shareholders, and potential for strong margins.

Market Opportunity

Growing Ghanaian air travel demand, under-served regional routes, and rising economic activity make this an ideal time to launch. Capture both domestic and ECOWAS markets.

Marketing & Branding

Ghanaian pride, social media campaigns, corporate partnerships, loyalty programs, and tourism promotion. Create a recognizable, trusted brand.

Risk Mitigation

Operational, financial, and market risks mitigated with insurance, governance, audits, and transparent reporting. Safety and contingency planning included.

Investor ROI

Equity growth, profit-sharing, and dividends. Long-term ownership aligned with Ghanaian economic growth. Shareholders participate in major decisions.
